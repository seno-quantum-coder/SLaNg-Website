# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers of SLaNg pledge to make participation in this project a respectful, productive, and harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, background, level of experience, nationality, or identity.

We are a small open-source project. We hold ourselves to a simple standard: treat people the way you would want to be treated if you were the newcomer asking a question for the first time.

---

## Our Standards

**Behavior that helps the project thrive:**

- Writing clear, constructive issue reports and pull request reviews
- Giving and receiving feedback graciously — critique the code, not the person
- Acknowledging when you are wrong or don't know something
- Helping newcomers get oriented without making them feel unwelcome
- Keeping discussions on-topic and technically focused

**Behavior that will not be tolerated:**

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks of any kind
- Dismissing contributions because of who someone is rather than what they wrote
- Deliberate intimidation or sustained disruption of discussions
- Publishing someone's private information without their consent
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider unprofessional or harmful

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, commit messages, discussions, and any other channel where project work happens. It also applies when someone is representing the project in public.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a private message to the project maintainer via GitHub. All reports will be reviewed promptly and kept confidential.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct, and to temporarily or permanently ban contributors whose behavior is harmful to the project.
